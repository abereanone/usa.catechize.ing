---
id: 187
title: Do electors have to vote for their party's candidate?
slug: pc-187
categories:
  - executive
authorId: jdhall
---

Neither the Constitution nor any federal law forces electors to vote for their party's candidate. However, some states require electors to vote for their party's candidate if the candidate wins a majority of votes from among citizens of that State. It is exceptionally rare - but not inconceivable - that an elector may vote contrary to the wishes of their party.
