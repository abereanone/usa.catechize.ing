---
id: 174
title: May any citizen of the United States receive a title of nobility from the king, or prince, or government of any other country?
slug: pc-174
categories:
  - powers
authorId: jdhall
---

The government does not interfere with the lives of private persons, but no person holding any office of profit or trust under our republic can accept either a title, sum of money as salary, an office, or even a gift, from any foreign throne or government, without the express consent of Congress.
