---
id: 78
title: Suppose all members of the Senate, or all members of the House of Representatives do not attend a meeting, can those who do not attend make laws without them?
slug: pc-78
categories:
  - legislature
authorId: jdhall
---

If more than one half are present, they have in most cases power to do whatever the whole number could have done. More than one half are called a majority, less than one half are called a minority. As many as are necessary to do business are called a quorum.
